Turning shopping into saving

Stash is an e-commerce experience that turns the act of checking out into an act of saving, helping compulsive shoppers build healthier habits without losing the thrill of checkout.

Role Product Designer
Timeline May–July 2025
Skills UX Research, UX Design, Interaction Design, Figma
The Problem

How might we capture the essence of online shopping without the guilt?

Interviewing self-described online shopping addicts revealed where existing e-commerce experiences often fall short.

Budget trackers

e.g. Mint, Monarch

Track and visualize spending, but only after money is already gone.

Automated savings tools

e.g. Qapital, Acorns

Moves money automatically in the background. Effective for saving, but doesn't address impulse shopping.

Proactive budgeting tool

Notable Outlier: YNAB

Helps plan spending before it happens, but still doesn't intervene at the point of purchase.

My solution

A tool that simulates an online shopping experience in order to support reckless spenders at the moment they feel the urge to shop.

I set out to validate this hypothesis by interviewing five self-identified online shopping addicts

My first instinct was to have tables change color as they approached and exceeded maximum capacity. However, that conflicted with the need to reserve color for registration statuses so they remained easily scannable, even in a tabling view.

This unique problem required a unique solution, and I ended up on a solution partially inspired by the stamina bar from The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild.
