Tournament registration made smarter

The existing tournament registration process at Golden Nugget Las Vegas required hours of manual balancing, so I redesigned it and introduced a seating experience that streamlined staff workflows.

Role Product Designer
Timeline May–August 2025
Skills UX Research, UX Design, Interaction Design, Figma, Claude Code, Lucidchart
The Problem

The first thing I learned about the Golden Nugget's Special Events team’s operations was how many problems their tournament registration tool was causing them

Pre-Event

Staff uploads invitee list received from Database Marketing department (40,000–60,000 rows)

This invitee list accounted for most guests attending the tournament, though some walk-ins did register on-site.

Registration Flow (repeated every round)

Staff select round

The "Event Name" dropdown contained every round (including mulligan-eligible rounds) and depended on a corporate IT contact to remove outdated entries

Staff records player information

Staff records each player's payment method before confirming their registration for the selected round.

Player draws a table & seat card

To distribute seating, players draw a random card from a basket that lists a table and seat number.

Post-Event

Staff export spreadsheets and manually balance them

At the end of the day, staff balance the sheets to ensure all exported data was accurate, a necessary step given how error-prone the current system was

Early explorations of the tabling system were really frustrating

My first instinct was to have tables change color as they approached and exceeded maximum capacity. However, that conflicted with the need to reserve color for registration statuses so they remained easily scannable, even in a tabling view.

This unique problem required a unique solution, and I ended up on a solution partially inspired by the stamina bar from The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild.
